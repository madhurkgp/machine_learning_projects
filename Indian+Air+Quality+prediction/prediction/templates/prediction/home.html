About Air Quality

What is Air Quality Index (AQI)?

The Air Quality Index is a numerical scale used to communicate the level of air pollution and its potential health effects. Higher values indicate worse air quality.

AQI Categories

  • Good (0-50): Air quality is satisfactory
  • Moderate (51-100): Acceptable for most people
  • Poor (101-200): Sensitive groups may experience health issues
  • Unhealthy (201-300): Everyone may experience health effects
  • Very Unhealthy (301-400): Health warnings of emergency conditions
  • Hazardous (401+): Emergency conditions affecting entire population

Pollutant Information

SO2 (Sulphur Dioxide): Mainly from burning fossil fuels, can cause respiratory issues

NO2 (Nitrogen Dioxide): From vehicle emissions and power plants, affects lung function

RSPM (Respirable Particles): Small particles that can penetrate deep into lungs

SPM (Suspended Particles): Total suspended particulate matter in the air
