About Voice Analysis for Parkinson's Disease

What We Measure

Our system analyzes various vocal characteristics including:

  • Fundamental Frequency: Average, maximum, and minimum vocal pitch
  • Jitter: Variations in fundamental frequency
  • Shimmer: Variations in amplitude
  • Harmonic Measures: Noise-to-harmonic ratios
  • Nonlinear Dynamics: Complexity measures of voice patterns

How It Works

Machine learning algorithms identify subtle patterns in voice characteristics that are often imperceptible to the human ear but correlate with Parkinson's disease progression.

The system uses a Decision Tree classifier trained on a dataset of voice recordings from both healthy individuals and Parkinson's patients.
