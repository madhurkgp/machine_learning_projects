About Parkinson's Disease Prediction

Learn about the science behind voice analysis for Parkinson's disease detection and how our machine learning system works.

What is Parkinson's Disease?

Parkinson's disease is a progressive neurodegenerative disorder that affects movement control. It occurs when nerve cells in the brain that produce dopamine become impaired or die.

Common symptoms include:

  • Tremors or shaking, especially in hands, arms, or legs
  • Stiffness of the limbs and trunk
  • Slowness of movement (bradykinesia)
  • Impaired balance and coordination
  • Changes in voice and speech patterns
Statistics
Approximately 1 million people in the US live with Parkinson's disease, with 60,000 new cases diagnosed each year.

Voice Analysis for Early Detection

Why Voice Matters

Up to 90% of people with Parkinson's disease experience voice and speech problems. These changes often occur early in the disease progression, making voice analysis a valuable tool for early detection.

Key Voice Indicators

  • Reduced Vocal Volume: Softer speaking voice
  • Monotone Speech: Loss of normal intonation
  • Hoarseness: Changes in voice quality
  • Rapid Speech:
    • Rushed or unclear speech patterns
  • Vocal Tremor: Shaking in the voice

Technical Analysis

Our system extracts and analyzes multiple acoustic features from voice recordings:

Frequency Analysis
Measures fundamental frequency patterns and variations
Amplitude Analysis
Analyzes voice intensity and shimmer patterns
Nonlinear Dynamics
Evaluates complexity and chaos in voice signals

Our Machine Learning Approach

Dataset

Our model is trained on the Oxford Parkinson's Disease Detection Dataset, which contains:

  • 195 voice recordings from 31 individuals
  • 23 people with Parkinson's disease
  • 8 healthy individuals
  • 22 acoustic features per recording

Algorithm

We use a Decision Tree classifier, which achieved:

  • 92.3% accuracy on test data
  • Perfect recall for healthy cases
  • High interpretability of decision rules

Feature Importance

The most influential features in our model include:

Spread1 (-8 to -2)
PPE (Pitch Period Entropy)
MDVP:Fo (Average Frequency)
DFA (Detrended Fluctuation)
RPDE (Recurrence Density)

Research & Scientific Background

This research builds on the work of Max Little et al. from the University of Oxford, who demonstrated that voice analysis could be an effective, non-invasive method for detecting Parkinson's disease.

"Voice recordings can be collected remotely and non-invasively, making them ideal for telemonitoring of Parkinson's disease progression."

Little MA, et al. IEEE Transactions on Biomedical Engineering, 2008

Key Research Findings

High Accuracy
Voice analysis can achieve 90%+ accuracy in distinguishing Parkinson's patients from healthy individuals
Early Detection
Voice changes often appear before motor symptoms, enabling earlier diagnosis
Remote Monitoring
Enables continuous monitoring of disease progression from home

Limitations & Future Directions

Current Limitations

  • Model trained on a relatively small dataset (195 samples)
  • Voice recordings must be collected in controlled environments
  • Does not account for other voice disorders
  • Requires specialized acoustic analysis equipment
  • Not suitable as a standalone diagnostic tool

Future Improvements

  • Expand dataset with more diverse participants
  • Implement deep learning models for better accuracy
  • Develop mobile app for easy voice recording
  • Include longitudinal tracking of disease progression
  • Integration with other biomarkers and clinical data
